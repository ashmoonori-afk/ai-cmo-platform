# Sample Client A 사용자 리서치 리포트 (User Research Report)
## Sample Client A — User Research Report

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Data sources:** Sample Client A WIKI (Notion), June 2025 Customer Survey (n=62, Sample Partner A flower purchasers), B2B client feedback (Sample Enterprise A, Sample Enterprise B, Sample Enterprise C, etc.), Product catalog & pricing, Customer rewards structure
**Sample size:** 62 survey respondents + qualitative B2B interviews
**Confidence level:** Medium — Survey data is from Sample Partner A users (skewed toward condolence purchasers), not Sample Client A direct customers. Treat personas and hypotheses as validated directions, not definitive conclusions.

---

## Executive Summary

Sample Client A serves two structurally distinct customer types: individual buyers who purchase reactively in response to life events (funeral notices, wedding invitations), and corporate buyers who manage recurring institutional obligations. The June 2025 survey confirms that **fast, reliable delivery and frictionless ordering are more important than price** — even though users say they want discounts when asked directly. The most underserved and highest-value segment is the **corporate executor**: an admin-role employee (총무/경리/구매담당) who makes repeat purchases, needs tax documentation, and is loyal by default but will switch if a competitor makes the process easier. Messaging that leads with "fast delivery guarantee + seamless billing" will outperform messaging that leads with price alone. The biggest growth lever is the **trigger moment** — most purchases begin from a KakaoTalk condolence or invitation notice link, meaning Sample Client A should position its touchpoint at the moment of notification rather than waiting for users to search.

## Personas

### Persona 1: 이소현 (Lee So-hyun) — "연락 받자마자 바로 주문하고 싶어요"
*"I want to order the moment I get the notice."*

- **Who:** Female, mid-30s, salaried professional. She receives 3–6 경조사 (life event) notifications per year via KakaoTalk from colleagues, university friends, and family. She doesn't think about flower vendors until a notification arrives.
- **Primary JTBD:** When I receive an unexpected funeral or wedding notice on KakaoTalk, I want to quickly order an appropriate wreath without having to research vendors, so I can fulfill my social obligation and move on with my day without anxiety.
- **Key pains:**
  1. Not knowing which product or price is "appropriate" for the relationship (과했나? 너무 적은 건 아닌가?)
  2. Not being able to verify whether the wreath was actually delivered on time
  3. Uncertainty about product quality — "will it look cheap?"
- **Key gains:** Peace of mind that the obligation is fulfilled, delivery confirmation photo, fast checkout (pay in under 2 minutes)
- **Behavioral pattern:** Discovers vendor through the link embedded in a 부고장/청첩장 or via Naver search. Chooses based on delivery speed promise and number of positive reviews. Buys once or twice a year. Rarely remembers the vendor afterward.
- **Prevalence:** ~55% of user base (individual purchasers)
- **Unexpected insight:** This persona is not price-driven in the moment of purchase — urgency overrides frugality. The 400% welcome coupon pack won't change her behavior; a delivery confirmation photo SMS would.

### Persona 2: 박준수 (Park Jun-su) — "실수 없이 처리하는 게 내 일이에요"
*"My job is to handle this without any mistakes."*

- **Who:** Male, early 40s, office manager or admin/general affairs (총무/구매) at a mid-size company. He handles the company's condolence and congratulatory wreath purchases as part of a broader admin role. He places 2–6 orders per month and needs clean accounting records.
- **Primary JTBD:** When a colleague or executive reports a condolence or business event, I want to order a wreath quickly through a vendor that provides proper tax invoices and reliable delivery tracking, so I can close the task without follow-up questions from accounting or my boss.
- **Key pains:**
  1. Vendors that don't issue proper tax invoices (세금계산서) or can't support post-payment billing (후불결제)
  2. Delivery failures or delays that create escalations with leadership
  3. Having to repeat the vendor search from scratch each time
- **Key gains:** Stable vendor relationship, automated billing at month/quarter end, delivery confirmation sent to him proactively
- **Behavioral pattern:** Uses Naver Smart Store or a pre-approved vendor list. High repeat rate. Willing to pay 10–15% more than retail for reliability and admin convenience. The decision-maker for switching is his manager or the executive team — so he needs a vendor that makes *him* look good.
- **Prevalence:** ~20% of user accounts, ~35–40% of GMV
- **Unexpected insight:** This persona is the highest LTV segment but is the least targeted by current messaging. The B2B rewards program targets executives (의사결정권자) with department store gift vouchers — but it's the executor who decides to *propose* switching vendors.

### Persona 3: 김태수 (Kim Tae-su) — "우리 회사 이름 걸고 보내는 건데"
*"This wreath is going out with our company's name on it."*

- **Who:** Male, late 40s–50s, business owner (대표) or senior executive (임원). He is simultaneously the purchase approver and sometimes the direct orderer for high-value or sensitive occasions — competitor funerals, major client anniversaries, large opening ceremonies.
- **Primary JTBD:** When a significant business relationship requires a floral gesture, I want a premium product that arrives on time and makes a strong impression, so I can maintain or strengthen the relationship without worrying about execution details.
- **Key pains:**
  1. Product quality inconsistency — the wreath looks nothing like the photo
  2. No way to confirm delivery without calling
  3. Feels like all flower vendors look the same; hard to distinguish quality
- **Key gains:** Premium presentation, proactive delivery confirmation, a vendor that feels like a "trusted partner" rather than a transaction
- **Behavioral pattern:** Rarely orders himself (usually delegates), but is the key influencer on vendor selection. Approves annual B2B contracts. Responds to personal relationship touches (handwritten notes, curated gift sets, direct account manager contact).
- **Prevalence:** ~10% of user accounts; drives the majority of high-value B2B contracts (annual spend ₩700K–₩10M+)
- **Unexpected insight:** The B2B rewards program's department store gift certificates are explicitly designed for this persona — but the survey shows he values quality reliability over discounts. "경조사에 보내는 화환인 만큼 가격이 비싸도 받는 사람이 고마움을 느낄 수 있는 퀄러티에 약속한 시간에 정확히 배송하는 것이 제일 중요." (Survey open-response, executive segment)

### Persona 4: 최지은 (Choi Ji-eun) — "우리 모임 대표로 챙기는 사람"
*"I'm the one who takes care of things for our group."*

- **Who:** Female, mid-40s–50s, de facto organizer of a social, alumni, or professional association (사적 모임). She is trusted by the group to handle collective gifting — condolence wreaths, congratulatory arrangements — and typically splits the cost via KakaoTalk group payments afterward.
- **Primary JTBD:** When someone in our network has a life event, I want to quickly coordinate a group contribution and send a meaningful gift, so the group is represented properly without the whole process falling on me alone.
- **Key pains:**
  1. Having to coordinate payment collection from members separately
  2. Unclear product options — doesn't know how to differentiate between "basic" and "premium"
  3. Feels personal responsibility for quality on behalf of the group
- **Key gains:** Group convenience features, ability to assign a group name on the card/ribbon, shared order history
- **Behavioral pattern:** Buys 4–8 times per year across multiple occasions. More likely to recommend a vendor she's used before. High referral potential if experience was positive.
- **Prevalence:** ~18% of user accounts
- **Unexpected insight:** This persona is the most natural referral vector — she's already coordinating group actions and communicating socially. Yet current referral mechanics (1:1 KakaoTalk invite) don't match her group-first mental model.

## Key Insights

**1. Delivery experience is the real product, not the flower.**
Survey data shows "fast/accurate delivery" (58%) and "order/payment convenience" (52%) outrank price (39%) and quality (21%) as purchase drivers. Users treat the wreath as a commodity and compete on logistics experience. Messaging that says "지금 주문, 오늘 배송 확정" will outperform "최대 30% 할인."

**2. The trigger moment is the KakaoTalk notice — not Naver.**
73% of respondents said they order through 부고장/청첩장 (condolence/invitation notice links). This means Sample Client A's most important distribution channel isn't search — it's being embedded in the moment of notification. Partnership with Sample Partner A (the condolence notification platform already connected to Sample Client B) is likely the single highest-leverage acquisition channel.

**3. Corporate executors are loyal by default — until the billing breaks.**
B2B customers (법인 목적) prioritized payment/billing convenience (65%) and admin processing (29%) far above the general population. Post-billing (후불결제) capability is a hard requirement for many corporate buyers. The PRD already in progress (후불결제) directly addresses the #1 B2B switching barrier.

**4. The "no complaints" response masks latent dissatisfaction.**
58% said they have no complaints. But open responses reveal they've simply lowered their expectations: "어쩌다가 이용하는거라 깊이 생각 안해봤음" and "실질적 혜택 체감한 적이 없음." This is an opportunity — users aren't loyal, they're just not actively searching for alternatives. A meaningfully better experience (photo confirmation, smart scheduling) could create genuine preference.

**5. Immediate discount beats everything, but it's not sticky.**
73% of respondents prefer immediate price discounts over points, tier benefits, or add-on services. This is consistent with a low-engagement category. However, the users who explained *why* they want discounts often said "because the products all look the same." The implication: if Sample Client A can differentiate the experience, discount dependency decreases.

**6. Group organizers are an untapped referral engine.**
The "사적 모임" segment (18%) represents people who already coordinate group actions — exactly the behavior Sample Client A needs to grow referrals. Current referral mechanics require 1:1 invitations, which doesn't match how this persona operates. A group order/link-sharing feature could activate this segment naturally.

## Recommendations

**1. Reframe positioning around the "moment of notice," not price.**
Current messaging should shift from discount-led to urgency/reliability-led: "Messaging Platform A톡 부고 받은 즉시 3분 주문, 당일 배송 보장." This directly addresses the #1 purchase driver (delivery certainty) and the #1 discovery channel (부고장 link).

**2. Make delivery photo confirmation a core feature, not a bonus.**
Multiple open-ended survey responses named photo confirmation as the single most-wanted new feature. If this is already live, promote it at checkout and in ads. If not, prioritize it ahead of loyalty program enhancements — it addresses both individual peace-of-mind and corporate accountability needs simultaneously.

**3. Fast-track 후불결제 (post-billing) to unlock the corporate segment.**
The corporate executor persona is the highest-LTV segment and is functionally blocked by billing requirements. The PRD is already in progress — this is the right call. Add "후불결제 가능" as a prominent message on all B2B acquisition touchpoints (cold emails, Naver B2B search, 복지몰 listings).

**4. Invest in the 경조사 캘린더 as a retention engine, not an event.**
The calendar feature currently has a ₩10,000 lottery incentive. Instead of lottery mechanics, treat calendar registration as the primary activation goal post-first-purchase: "언제 또 필요할지 모르니, 미리 등록해두면 알림으로 알려드릴게요." Target the 40% D7 activation KPI through calendar registration rather than forcing a second order.

**5. Build a group order flow to activate the 모임 대표형 segment.**
A simple "단체 주문 링크 공유" feature — where one person sets up the order and others can contribute payment — would match how this persona already behaves and unlock the referral flywheel more naturally than the current 1:1 KakaoTalk invite mechanic.

**6. Separate B2B messaging by executor vs. decision-maker.**
Current B2B rewards are designed for executives (department store vouchers) but the executor persona (총무/구매) is the actual switching decision-maker at most companies. Add executor-specific messaging: "정산 걱정 없이, 한 번 등록하면 알아서 처리됩니다" to address their core JTBD.

## Open Questions

These are hypotheses the current data cannot answer — recommended follow-up research:

1. **What happens to individual buyers between events?** Do they remember Sample Client A at all? What would trigger re-engagement before the next 경조사? (Suggested: longitudinal cohort study or targeted interview with 3+ order customers)

2. **Why is the sign-up → first purchase conversion stuck at 15%?** Is it price friction, trust, product selection anxiety, or session abandonment? (Suggested: funnel analytics + 5 usability interviews on the checkout flow)

3. **How do corporate executor personas discover new vendors?** Is it Naver, peer recommendation, or is it entirely dictated by executives? (Suggested: 10 B2B cold interviews targeting 총무/구매 personas)

4. **What is the actual satisfaction rate with delivery experiences?** Survey shows 58% "no complaints" but this is a stated preference, not behavioral data. (Suggested: post-delivery NPS micro-survey, automated 24hrs after delivery)

5. **Is the Sample Partner A integration the strongest acquisition channel?** The data suggests it is, but Sample Client A's own funnel data would confirm relative contribution. (Suggested: UTM-tagged channel attribution analysis)
